Music Lessons and the Community

Qualitative Research for the MusicLink Foundation

"Any child who has musical potential deserves the opportunity to nurture this talent... The MusicLink Foundation reaches out to low-income families by linking these students with professional music teachers willing to reduce their fee."

As a doctoral student at George Mason University, I worked with a team to conduct interviews and analyze qualitative data for MusicLink Foundation. The goal was to evaluate the impact of the program on students, teachers, and families.

Methodology

I interviewed former students, teachers, and parents using remote interview techniques. I transcribed these interviews and used line-by-line coding to identify initial categories.

Using constant comparison and focused coding, I created post-it notes for key data points and organized them on an Affinity Wall.

Key Findings

1. The Teacher-Student Bond

MusicLink was the primary reason music existed in the lives of many families. The teacher/student dynamic led to trust, organizational strategies, and a life-long love of music.

"I didn't like her, so I took from her and then I quit... I started again when I was 9 and I had that teacher then all the way through college."

2. Longitudinal Effects

Participants reported improved creativity, self-discipline, and self-confidence attributed to lessons. These effects often manifested years later.

"...sometimes your impact, you don't realize for 20 years."

Conclusion

MusicLink's process of identifying and developing musical talent works well. Future strategies should focus on ensuring the right teacher-student match and celebrating the life-long skills developed through this mentorship.
