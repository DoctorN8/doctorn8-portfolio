Creating a Vocal Rubric

for District IX of VMEA

Problem

Teachers were frustrated that the students were not receiving appropriate feedback on their auditions. In some cases, the adjudicators would contradict each other. It was clear that the audition form needed to be amended to improve the experience of the adjudicators and the singers.

Solution

I created a proposed vocal adjudication rubric. This was submitted and approved for use. However, changes to the event structure migrated the process online and the district ultimately moved in a different direction.

Methodolog​ies

Interviews

I conducted user interviews to hear what teachers were experiencing as they used the current form. I prepared an interview script with 10 open-ended questions, focusing on adjudicator experience on audition days. Over the course of several weeks, I recruited and interviewed 6 teachers and adjudicators. We referenced the user interview findings throughout the entire design process.

Card Sorting

I did a qualitative analysis of the interview notes to identify patterns and similarities. I wanted to make sure that the form I created aligned with user expectations. The goal was to find intuitive groupings for the interview notes. After organizing the data I discovered the following:

  • The most important aspect was appropriate feedback for the singer.

  • Veteran teachers opposed change because they believed one would simply "know" within a few seconds if the singer should be selected.

  • 35% of the total score should be based on the sight-reading example.

  • Time is a constraint with only 30-90 seconds between singers.

Lo-Fi Prototyping

I began the design process with low-fidelity prototypes using Google Suite. Prototypes were based on the initial user interviews, constraints, and the comparative evaluation. Each of these pointed to the fact that users needed to communicate a lot of information in not a lot of time. I came back to these prototypes throughout the entire design process to make sure that I did not lose sight of the primary goals and ideas.

Next Steps

01

Updates to many school division non-discrimination clauses mean that this form should be updated to include non-binary pronouns.

04

Conduct usability testing to ensure that the proposed rubric is effective in achieving the intended goals. This would involve observing how adjudicators and singers interact with the rubric in a mock audition setting, and making adjustments based on feedback.

02

Investigation should be made to moving this document to an online environment. When the client decided to migrate the audition process online, this document was all but forgotten.

05

Consider incorporating technology such as voice recognition software or video recording to enhance the feedback process and reduce time constraints. This could allow adjudicators to provide more detailed feedback to singers, while also streamlining the process.

03

Follow up with individual teachers and or locations that are using this form in house. That would provide some insights into the usability of the document.

06

Regularly review and update the rubric to ensure that it remains relevant and effective in meeting the needs of all users. This could involve conducting additional user research and testing, and making adjustments based on feedback and changing needs.
